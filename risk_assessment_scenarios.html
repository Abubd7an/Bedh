Working at Height
العمل على ارتفاع
Height HIGH ▼
What is the hazard?Working at height on scaffolding, ladders, or leading edges above 2 metres.
Who might be harmed?Workers on the scaffold, operatives below, members of the public nearby.
How might people be harmed?Workers may fall from height causing serious injury or death. Tools or materials may fall and strike people below.
Existing controls
  • Scaffold erected and inspected by competent person weekly
  • Edge protection (guardrails, toe boards) installed
  • Workers wear safety harnesses for leading-edge work
  • Hard hats mandatory in all areas below working at height
  • Toolbox talk on working at height conducted
Additional controls
  • Daily visual check of scaffold before use
  • Exclusion zone established below all elevated work
  • Debris nets or fans installed on scaffold
  • Rescue plan in place for fallen or suspended worker
Residual risk ratingLOW — Risk is acceptable with controls in place. Continue to monitor.

Excavation & Trenching
الحفر والخنادق
Excavation HIGH ▼
What is the hazard?Collapse of excavation walls, underground services (gas, electricity, water), flooding, and engulfment.
Who might be harmed?Excavation workers, plant operators, anyone working near the trench edge.
How might people be harmed?Soil collapse can bury and suffocate workers. Striking a live cable can cause electrocution. Flooding can trap workers.
Existing controls
  • Services survey and CAT scan completed before excavation
  • Shoring, battering, or benching applied to trenches over 1.2m
  • Competent person inspects excavation daily
  • Safe access/egress (ladders) provided every 6 metres
  • Spoil kept minimum 0.5m from trench edge
Additional controls
  • Barriers and warning signs around excavation perimeter
  • Emergency rescue equipment available on site
  • Gas detector used before entry in confined sections
  • No plant to operate within 3m of trench edge without assessment
Residual risk ratingMEDIUM — Ongoing monitoring required. Inspect after every rainfall or ground disturbance.

Electrical Hazards
المخاطر الكهربائية
Electrical HIGH ▼
What is the hazard?Contact with live electrical cables (underground or overhead), faulty equipment, or unprotected temporary supplies.
Who might be harmed?All site operatives, electricians, plant operators working near overhead lines.
How might people be harmed?Electric shock causing cardiac arrest or burns. Striking a buried cable can cause explosion and fire. Overhead line contact can kill instantly.
Existing controls
  • Services survey completed — buried cables marked
  • All temporary electrical equipment PAT tested
  • 110V reduced voltage system used on site
  • RCDs (residual current devices) installed on all circuits
  • Only qualified electricians work on electrical systems
Additional controls
  • Goal posts and height restriction barriers under overhead lines
  • Lockout/tagout procedure for isolation before maintenance
  • Daily inspection of cables, leads, and connections
  • Emergency plan for electrocution posted at site office
Residual risk ratingLOW — Risk reduced with isolation procedures and reduced voltage system.

Manual Handling
المناولة اليدوية / الرفع اليدوي
Manual MEDIUM ▼
What is the hazard?Lifting, carrying, pushing, or pulling heavy or awkward loads such as blocks, steel bars, bags of cement, or timber.
Who might be harmed?Labourers, bricklayers, steel fixers, and any worker lifting materials manually.
How might people be harmed?Musculoskeletal injuries — back injuries, sprains, hernias. Dropping loads onto feet. Long-term chronic back pain.
Existing controls
  • Manual handling training provided at induction
  • Mechanical aids (forklifts, hoists, trolleys) used where possible
  • Team lifts organised for loads over 20kg
  • Safety footwear worn at all times
Additional controls
  • Materials stored at waist height where possible to avoid bending
  • Regular rest breaks for repetitive tasks
  • Workers encouraged to report pain or discomfort early
  • Occupational health review for workers with existing back problems
Residual risk ratingLOW — Risk acceptable with mechanical aids and correct technique training.

Plant & Vehicle Movement
حركة الآلات والمركبات
Plant HIGH ▼
What is the hazard?Moving excavators, dumpers, cranes, and delivery vehicles on site colliding with pedestrian workers.
Who might be harmed?Ground workers, delivery drivers, site visitors, workers crossing vehicle routes.
How might people be harmed?Workers struck or run over by moving plant — causing crush injuries or death. Overturning plant can trap operators.
Existing controls
  • Segregated pedestrian and vehicle routes established
  • Banksman (signaller) used when reversing all large plant
  • Hi-vis vests worn by all site personnel
  • Plant operators hold valid CPCS/NPORS licences
  • Plant pre-use checks completed and recorded daily
Additional controls
  • Speed limit of 10 mph enforced across site
  • Pedestrian exclusion zones around crane operating areas
  • CCTV cameras on reversing plant where banksman view is limited
  • Site induction includes plant/pedestrian segregation routes
Residual risk ratingMEDIUM — Strict site traffic management plan to be followed at all times.

Fire & Hot Works
الحريق وأعمال اللهب
Fire HIGH ▼
What is the hazard?Hot works (welding, cutting, grinding) igniting flammable materials. Arson. Spontaneous combustion of waste materials.
Who might be harmed?All site personnel, neighbouring properties and public.
How might people be harmed?Burns, smoke inhalation, structural collapse due to fire. Explosions from gas cylinders if exposed to heat.
Existing controls
  • Hot works permit issued before any welding or cutting
  • 1-hour fire watch after all hot works completed
  • Flammable materials removed or protected within 3m of hot works
  • Fire extinguishers positioned at hot work locations
  • Emergency evacuation procedure displayed and communicated
Additional controls
  • Fire assembly point clearly signed and known to all
  • Site security to prevent arson risk out of hours
  • Gas cylinders stored upright, chained, in ventilated cage
  • Fire risk assessment reviewed monthly
Residual risk ratingLOW — With hot works permit system and fire watch, risk is well controlled.

Fuel / Chemical Spill
انسكاب الوقود والمواد الكيميائية
Environment MEDIUM ▼
What is the hazard?Spillage of diesel, oil, cement washout, or chemicals during storage, refuelling, or handling operations.
Who might be harmed?Site workers exposed to chemicals. Environment — watercourses, soil, wildlife.
How might people be harmed?Skin or eye burns from chemical contact. Environmental contamination can result in prosecution and project shutdown.
Existing controls
  • COSHH assessments completed for all chemicals on site
  • Bunded storage for all fuel tanks (110% containment)
  • Spill kit available at fuel storage and refuelling points
  • All drains covered before concrete or washout operations
  • Workers trained on spill response procedure
Additional controls
  • Refuelling to take place in designated drip-proof area only
  • Chemical inventory reviewed monthly
  • Environment Agency emergency number posted on site
  • Concrete washout area designated — no washout near drains
Residual risk ratingLOW — Bunded storage and spill kits reduce risk significantly.

Dust & Respiratory Hazards
الغبار ومخاطر الجهاز التنفسي
Health MEDIUM ▼
What is the hazard?Inhalation of concrete dust, silica dust (RCS), wood dust, or asbestos fibres during cutting, grinding, or drilling.
Who might be harmed?Concreters, joiners, bricklayers, demolition workers — all workers in dusty environments.
How might people be harmed?Long-term lung diseases: silicosis, asbestosis, COPD, and lung cancer. Symptoms may not appear for many years.
Existing controls
  • COSHH assessment completed for all dusty activities
  • Water suppression used during cutting and grinding
  • On-tool extraction (vacuum) used on power tools
  • FFP3 dust masks provided for high-risk tasks
  • Asbestos survey completed before any demolition
Additional controls
  • Enclosed or screened areas for particularly dusty operations
  • Regular air monitoring in confined spaces
  • Health surveillance programme (annual lung function checks)
  • Wet cutting preferred over dry cutting at all times
Residual risk ratingLOW — With RPE and wet suppression, risk is controlled. Monitor via health surveillance.

Crane & Lifting Operations
عمليات الرافعة والرفع
Plant HIGH ▼
What is the hazard?Failure of crane, slings, or lifting accessories. Load swinging or dropping. Overloading of crane capacity.
Who might be harmed?Crane operator, slingers/signallers, workers and public in the crane's operating radius.
How might people be harmed?Dropped load causing crush injuries or death. Crane collapse or overturning can kill multiple people.
Existing controls
  • Lift plan prepared by appointed person for all crane lifts
  • Crane inspected by competent person — thorough examination certificate current
  • All lifting accessories inspected before each use
  • Certified slinger/signaller on all lifts
  • Exclusion zone established under all suspended loads
Additional controls
  • Critical lifts (complex or near boundaries) require specific lift plan approval
  • Wind speed monitoring — lifting suspended if wind exceeds safe limit
  • Two-way radios for communication between operator and signaller
  • Anti-collision device on tower cranes where multiple cranes operate
Residual risk ratingMEDIUM — Risk remains significant. Strict lift plan compliance is essential on every lift.

Confined Space Entry
الدخول إلى الأماكن المغلقة
Confined HIGH ▼
What is the hazard?Entry into manholes, tanks, tunnels, or deep excavations with risk of oxygen deficiency, toxic gases, or engulfment.
Who might be harmed?Any worker entering the confined space and rescuers if the entry goes wrong.
How might people be harmed?Asphyxiation from lack of oxygen. Poisoning from toxic gases (H₂S, CO). Drowning or engulfment. Many rescuers die trying to save the original victim.
Existing controls
  • Permit to work (confined space) signed before every entry
  • Atmospheric testing before and during entry (O₂, CO, H₂S, LEL)
  • Trained confined space entrant and standby person required
  • Breathing apparatus and rescue equipment on standby
  • Tripod and lifeline used for all vertical entries
Additional controls
  • Confined space rescue team briefed and available before entry
  • Communication maintained throughout — no lone working
  • Non-sparking tools used in flammable atmospheres
  • Entry duration limited — regular atmospheric re-testing
Residual risk ratingMEDIUM — Never attempt rescue without correct equipment. Avoid entry unless absolutely necessary.

Heat Stress & Cold Stress
الإجهاد الحراري وإجهاد البرد
Health MEDIUM ▼
What is the hazard?Working outdoors in extreme heat (summer) or extreme cold (winter), or working near heat sources (boilers, hot work).
Who might be harmed?All outdoor workers — particularly those in heavy PPE, doing strenuous physical work.
How might people be harmed?Heat stroke, dehydration, and loss of consciousness in hot conditions. Hypothermia, frostbite, and reduced concentration in cold conditions — increasing accident risk.
Existing controls
  • Shade structures or rest areas provided in hot weather
  • Free drinking water available at all work locations
  • Warm welfare facilities (heated drying room) in cold weather
  • Work schedule adjusted during extreme temperature alerts
Additional controls
  • Weather monitoring — toolbox talk if extreme conditions forecast
  • Buddy system in extreme conditions — workers check on each other
  • Signs and symptoms of heat stroke/hypothermia communicated to all
  • First aiders trained in heat/cold illness response
Residual risk ratingLOW — With hydration, rest breaks, and welfare facilities, risk is manageable.

Noise & Vibration
الضوضاء والاهتزاز
Health/Env MEDIUM ▼
What is the hazard?Exposure to high noise levels from machinery, and hand-arm vibration from power tools (drills, breakers, compactors).
Who might be harmed?Workers operating noisy plant or vibrating tools. Neighbours and public near the site boundary.
How might people be harmed?Permanent noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL). Hand-arm vibration syndrome (HAVS) — permanent numbness and loss of grip. Neighbour complaints and legal action.
Existing controls
  • Noise risk assessment completed — exposure levels measured
  • Hearing protection zones established and signed
  • Ear defenders provided at 85dB and mandatory at 87dB
  • Noisy operations scheduled during permitted hours only
  • HAVS exposure points tracked for all tool users
Additional controls
  • Quieter equipment specified at procurement stage
  • Anti-vibration gloves provided for prolonged tool use
  • Health surveillance (hearing tests, HAVS questionnaire) annually
  • Noise barriers erected near site boundaries if required
Residual risk ratingLOW — With hearing protection and HAVS tracking, long-term health risk is minimised.
